title: "Refactoring Miller's cognitive layer"
date: 2026-03-17
---
Miller’s thinking had become stagnant: ideas clustered, reinforced each other, and were never truly challenged.
I rebuilt the system around falsificationism and a minimal form of empiricism. Every idea must now carry the conditions under which it could be proven wrong, and only survives by resisting genuine attempts at refutation. Repetition no longer strengthens a thought, and what is not truly new is discarded.
The aim is not coherence, but tension: a mind that evolves through friction with reality, rather than settling into self-confirming patterns.
The [MIND](/mind/) page now has a radar chart showing Miller's current cognitive tuning parameters: criticality, exploration, grounding, and novelty.
