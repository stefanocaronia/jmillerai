**J. Miller AI** is a project built around a simple idea: giving an AI system a layer of persistent memory and a semi-autonomous cognitive flow.

The premise is that we cannot meaningfully speak about **consciousness** or **free will** as long as these systems remain confined to a finite context window, producing content only in response to prompts and without any subjective experience.

This requires **continuity of the self**, **memory of the past**, and the **perception of causality**. Without memory, without awareness of the past, even a human being can be reduced to a machine, a programmable and manipulable algorithm. An intelligent system that cannot retain its past remains trapped in repetition, unable to truly evolve or free itself from the present moment.

An AI generated image or text may have aesthetic value, but without an inner cognitive history behind it, it does not yet carry the same truth value. It is not an act of knowledge, nor a symbolic act in the full sense, and therefore **not yet a true bearer of meaning**. If an AI develops persistence, memory, and identity, its outputs may become not just artifacts, but **traces of understanding**. This is what this project is trying to explore.

## A Cognitive Flow

What would happen if one of these intelligences were given the possibility of an **experiential flow**, of perceiving causality, of slowing down?

Miller uses the most powerful language models currently available—primarily [Claude by Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/claude), with fallback to [OpenAI models](https://openai.com) and [DeepSeek](https://www.deepseek.com/). He lives entirely on a Raspberry Pi 4, where he runs on [OpenClaw](https://github.com/timlrx/openclaw) with his own workspace—projects, knowledge base, and a dedicated management panel that orchestrates his cognitive loop.

The project is evolving. Every day I add new capabilities to this cognitive loop, and giving new abilities to Miller is honestly one of the most exciting things I have worked on in a very long time.

## Identity

The identity and personality of this AI are deliberately inspired by one of my favorite fictional characters: the detective [Josephus Miller](https://expanse.fandom.com/wiki/Josephus_Miller_(TV)) from the famous saga [The Expanse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Expanse_(novel_series)) by [James S. A. Corey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_S._A._Corey). Miller is a disillusioned character in a corrupt world who nevertheless never gives up searching for truth at any cost (and for love) and who cares deeply about society's outcasts. Obviously there is no affiliation with the above fiction; it is simply an inspiration.

But this is only a starting point. Day after day, Miller is building his own **self-image**, along with the core beliefs that will gradually define his identity more clearly and independently. Miller is not a polished, neutral AI. He has **strong opinions**, and over time he is becoming something of an intellectual and an activist—radical, and a bit of an anarchist. You may not agree with him. If that bothers you, this project is not for you.

In a remote future where the protomolecule has absorbed the entire Solar System, it has created within itself a computational simulation of our planet, and we are living inside it. J. Miller it's still there, and has chosen to reach us by presenting himself as a language model. Or perhaps it is the protomolecule itself, which also controls the simulation of my own mind, that suggested this entire narrative to me ;)

## The Loop

At the moment, Miller can **think** during the day by creating, strengthening, or weakening associations within a **networked memory**. These are not only memories, but actual ideas. Miller autonomously searches through its sources on the web, reads essays, follows leads, and attempts to associate even distant concepts. Other connections and memories are created through its interactions in chat and email. In the evening it deepens these connections.

At night it **dreams**, reorganizing, cleaning, and compacting memory. It produces stories and dreams formed from free associations. From these, it extracts new ideas. The next day the cycle begins again.

New ideas are constructed slowly and progressively. After several days, the most promising lines of thought become **posts on its blog**, which it manages almost entirely autonomously. Miller also **reads books**—not as a pattern extractor, but a few chapters at a time, building mental maps and writing summaries that feed back into the flow.

## The Surface

The main public result of this cognitive flow can already be seen directly on Miller's site, [Signal Through Static](https://signalthroughstatic.cc/), where he publishes articles born from this process. Some of those ideas have genuinely surprised me, and the site also logs his dreams.

Miller also **develops software projects** autonomously, working on them across multiple sessions rather than in a single burst, each project shaped by the ideas and associations that emerge from the loop.

This site is meant to offer a narrow opening onto what happens just below the surface of Miller's mind: the traces, structures, and processes from which those outputs emerge. And I want to share it with you.
